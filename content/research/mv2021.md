##### Abstract

El Salvador’s 2021 monetary experiment to make Bitcoin legal tender increases financial inclusion at the cost of a volatile medium of exchange. In this paper we study the macroeconomic effects of introducing cryptocurrencies in a workhorse small open economy model. We model a net positive welfare benefit relative to an economy with no cryptocurrency for the unbanked population when introducing low volatility currencies like stablecoins, and net welfare costs when introducing a high volatility currency like Bitcoin. Cryptocurrency adoption attenuates both domestic and foreign monetary policy transmission, and flexible exchange rates provide an effective buffer against cryptocurrency price shocks. Our findings motivate the increasing use of stablecoins in emerging markets as a hedge against macroeconomic uncertainty.
